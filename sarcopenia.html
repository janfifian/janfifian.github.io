SRTSI and Sarcopenia Screening

Considering the frequent coexistence of heart failure, glomerular filtration rate (GFR) calculated using the Cockcroft–Gault formula, body mass index (BMI), and an empirically determined correction coefficient α, the following index—RTSI (Rapid Test for Sarcopenia Screening Indications)—was developed. The RTSI is intended to support early identification of sarcopenia risk based on routine laboratory measurements and basic anthropometric data.

RTSI Formula

\[ \mathrm{RTSI} = \log\!\left( \frac{\mathrm{NTproBNP}}{\mathrm{GFR}\cdot(\mathrm{BMI}-\alpha)^2} \right) \]

Where:

  • NTproBNP – NT-proBNP concentration [pg/ml]
  • GFR – creatinine clearance calculated using Cockcroft–Gault
  • BMI – body mass index [kg/m²]
  • α – correction coefficient; in this study α = 15 kg/m²

Simplified Index (SRTSI)

To simplify calculations, a predictive value analysis was performed for a modified RTSI index calculated using serum creatinine, which is among the most routinely obtained biochemical parameters in screening laboratory tests. The simplified index was defined as SRTSI (Simplified Rapid Test for Sarcopenia Screening Indications).

\[ \mathrm{SRTSI} = \log\!\left( \frac{\mathrm{NTproBNP}}{\mathrm{K}\cdot(\mathrm{BMI}-\alpha)^2} \right) \]

Where:

  • NTproBNP – NT-proBNP concentration [pg/ml]
  • K – serum creatinine [mg/dl]
  • BMI – body mass index [kg/m²]
  • α – correction coefficient; in this study α = 15 kg/m²

Key Findings

  • NT-proBNP as a promising biomarker for sarcopenia: A statistically significant elevation of NT-proBNP concentrations was observed in patients with sarcopenia (mean 1060.5 pg/ml) compared with the control group (572.8 pg/ml) (p = 0.02), confirming an association between this marker and sarcopenia.
  • Limitations of current screening: The recommended SARC-F questionnaire demonstrated low sensitivity (0.50 in the overall study population; 0.0 in the subgroup without frailty), highlighting its limited utility for early sarcopenia risk detection.
  • SRTSI – a novel index with high diagnostic accuracy: The simplified SRTSI index, based on NT-proBNP, creatinine concentration, and BMI, showed very good diagnostic performance in the entire cohort (AUC = 0.82), reaching an AUC of 0.87 in patients without heart or kidney failure.
  • Improved clinical sensitivity: A proposed cut-off value of SRTSI ≥ 1 for the general population yielded a sensitivity of 0.73 and a specificity of 0.75.
  • Practical applicability: Owing to its simplicity and reliance on widely available biochemical parameters, SRTSI may be readily implemented in general practice to facilitate earlier sarcopenia risk stratification.

RTSI/SRTSI are screening tools intended to support clinical decision-making. They were established in the works of Marcin Radkowski and Filip Turoboś. We recommend that the final diagnosis of sarcopenia should be based on established diagnostic criteria.
